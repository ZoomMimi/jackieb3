I went with a group of ladies to the National Quilt Museum. The quilts were amazing. This is a sample of some of my favorites. It's amazing what these quilters can do with pieces of fabric.The red, white and blue was from an exhibit of quilts made for veterans. the Christmas tree is a close up of one square on a larger quilt. Quite an interesting exhibit of talent.

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjWt2lXMkZRMpfU2uJVLX_QzxUMv0i0v-FbbFMqAFcDejFFQJolzSOHAVRw9w9IYJg4zFqSpnfUEhqCtq1CSFJpFP57burzQtKKtUzoRTQ_oDXFym5pohA6rOXfdjtPjI8U3yXBmspmadhcEcdRy-kTi7jcKw81sMNkcJkYLet3h3DEiFahAaAHBaODNHA/s954/43715378-C1B6-4B05-A9A7-3700E3559400.jpeg)

This was a super cute town. Reminded us of New Bern. In addition to the quilt museum they have a river history museum, many restaurants, a wine bar a distillery a brewery, a bakery, a sweet shop, and theatre venues.

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEgE9brQh9SXQfkKVojaptHGdkg85SkBOoEeDSpCjULrKyJBQRjPBlrBCeugmzvAEf3W6sO2EePBNxDte8WkwozxUoxkZCgTEKGCtMmn-5SGHNcg0EIZAD0R5kgBVpSE0JskcEh7FwsZzDC3c45hbgtTOnbsI5RBFUi5uUrzDtd6x1zIHofK3YZX20P9YMs/s903/C12F0A76-845E-4267-BBB8-3C5FE9F39B40.jpeg)

This theatre was originally a market. The mural to the right shows its original use.

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEgcYXfj3JRinuJgZGCRVwinH2uFLfekPLyQE8kGpk5MEOd5g7seD7lKxcyhl21FM1yMYfIeyFjjsxaTqTBsDSnrkJJW5sIthRDFYJ-r4DgPnsOVlOwR7vt36xGGWdWkzkgyhuSUO6TehYOcgFb2SoUYgRi0abmmIAMCrE2mEE8wDggwufvksQFs_C0jWIg/s960/14F665AF-C7C9-4AA3-AF61-FA7AE8EB2B73.jpeg)

The city is protected from the Ohio river by a flood wall. There are murals depicting the locations history which was very interesting.

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEiJh3y1nnfMA3kUdYDlFLNKua48X-cySZhc0OfAy32Jdi0x5ng0i1HjP0aTbDCIj-eLEV9gydTXXuAKz30G3KlKcUl1hd9n8UVoQDkpv2yJD31_oez-yEJ1EDdRbqg9ef8A34EUWmC7lz5Kr_7_zbkeEm4aZYWwdkfnnl1dYH8UTScSnqSm9n0LzqhNtB0/s4032/3E9DD69E-6C32-40F1-BFA1-F0DCD9F217B6.jpeg)

This was the largest mural; took up three sections. This town has put a lot into their waterfront community bringing it back in a wonderful way. Definitely worth a stop. I wish we had stayed for two days in stead of one.
