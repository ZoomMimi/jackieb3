We rode our bicycles into Key West on our first full day here. We went to Divers Direct to learn about the Brownie Air system for diving then we went to lunch at the Wharf. This is where we saw our first Manatee since being in Florida.

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEj4gd9mhWDUi2jnMYUi1J40goqoszAyvqycC_ntsTgvdJU2GFPmwR9_xJqi_0iUa-hBDGXd0GJnZKM_9mPosqUR_ZfPeSHw6TvJwujDyA0bWw1JTLP12vZjzqKqADm4CcOrqzatcc4-92mDJ0qn-ZpB2igpn4fa_jfAfNOZ75OPPdIypJPr_m-0NII4V-U/w612-h458/0A864092-BD13-44EB-8718-8718B9AE68F3.jpeg)

The Tarpon were also numerous and quite active feeding on the fishermen's cleaning remains.

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEh9cMNTdA_Z6e6jBXexYdCbxqjrymn39C7L8_5s_15PUnx30QIHBNErYLM4LowvqdpRq87gqjtrx4es1exyauZnBjgozRkMhtQ0FoyDDGSooU9q-TOpRlTEuSQ_DuBxFruLoG9n5qFTdqYxSDMu4eILLQ86x0qQAg8bYKzBCs8jKrLPU6BhQmT_mguFI0g/w610-h458/199C0139-A873-4773-85B8-2EA537B6E934.jpeg)

We were thrilled and honored to greet our friends Jerry and Denice on Nevadatude at the dock where they crossed their wake. We had a lovely celebration with them and a number of other Loopers. We couldn't stay long because we needed to pick up provisions and ride out bikes back out to Boca Chica. We were pleasantly surprised with a dinner invitation from our Looper friends on Hearken. They felt sorry for us after our long bike ride back to the marina (seven miles).

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjtlPp3jchl7mditpPAb8E21kcfPPlMObtyOU8uALXghgVgFyKiKAA7-RCbhooT4lRcRsJwBdjaFJYYPWxaVP1BhX40j4g5J6pEwH92hbGgz8BZ9FiTUX1yOQMikarVPxxRF8FwKfMDGhaWe1MgWTpc1v_8pgWTD_oBomh89uqaclv3FhVp7PJGw1yG9Jg/w599-h449/E8F17A4B-FF62-432B-B01D-FB60E48173BC.jpeg)

Our next trip into Key West was by dinghy. We rafted to Nevadatude and walked into town with our wagon to purchase the Brownie. That was much easier than a long bike ride to and from the marina.
