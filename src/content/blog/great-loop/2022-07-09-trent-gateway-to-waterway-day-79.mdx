Beautiful Calm day for cruising out ahead of us you can see one of the many car ferries crossing the waterway.

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEgZQkQiIOl8eaMLhJDmWXrHcsVZmp6AVO0vMepKJmZGOYw_SlvHy2sYOCUwrD5G9CrX2TGWPSUvh4U0uDUztXNzgXKSIRu1GRRYoW4-sjZOTy-PA4_O8-Swk0TvY8EkykBCU4-9_wxDQXGbETh2JojtF6SloZqFpGSAfA0toN5yg2YRfUWFWonpp56a/w565-h241/DB76A95F-5810-43E4-87F9-4B2D68CE1623.jpeg)

The day was uneventful, but we did see an island covered with cormorants and lots of swans. I saw so many pairs of swans with babies but they were too far away to capture with my iPhone camera. This lone swan was so bold to cross right in front of the boat.

![](https://blogger.googleusercontent.com/img/b/R29vZ2xl/AVvXsEjLwtxwRNYQ-m0MXQV4Wzt-LDKp-uWgpQC-qO_yYQS8Wm7U3fSkXVG1OVmK5eIQ7kGStsJv3jL_UzAwOTL6R-sux3xM6Qm0tqjASoNL3In2FmVfF5RDqtwIYDuvVBgEXDI8hHxwBx3J0z0hG2lKPrqSdhggM3HocFOkzgIlDTsBcz_YXXqsjM5hFoh5/w551-h209/474338FA-E0CC-43DB-909E-581ECD1FDEC1.jpeg)

The Trent Port Marina was a top notch facility. The restrooms were individual rooms with very clean showers. They had a fireplace and computers in the lounge for our use. The washers and dryers were very new, free, and they even provided the soap. The best thing about this marina was the proximity to a large grocery store. We gathered provisions and got ready to begin the Trent-Severn Waterway.
